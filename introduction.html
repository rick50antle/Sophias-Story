Meet Sofia

An optional introduction to the story

Every organization begins with an idea. Sofia's came on a trail in the Rocky Mountains.

She was an engineer by training, a tinkerer by temperament. She had hiked these trails before, but this time she noticed something she hadn't thought much about: how hard it was for hikers and climbers to find safe, environmentally friendly portable water filtration. Most options were either expensive or wasteful. Some were both. She stopped at a stream crossing, watched the water run clear over the rocks, and started sketching in the margin of her trail map. A compact filter. Biodegradable materials. Something that purifies water as you drink it.

By the time she reached the trailhead that evening, the sketch had become an idea, and the idea had already split in two. She could sell these filters to backpackers and outdoor enthusiasts—people like herself who would pay for something well-designed. But she could also provide them to communities in water-stressed regions, places where clean drinking water was not a convenience but a daily uncertainty. One idea, two purposes. She did not yet know how consequential that split would become.
